# PRODUCT.md — Run4Ricky Foundation

register: brand

## Product purpose

A story-driven charity site for the Run4Ricky Foundation. It exists to convert
strangers into supporters of spinal cord injury (SCI) research and inclusion, by
telling one true story with enough force that people donate, share, or find help.

The site is the product. A visitor's impression IS the deliverable.

## The story (the spine of the site)

Riccardo "Ricky" Barbieri fractured C4/C5 falling headfirst into a lake in the
Omani mountains, December 2022. Coma, a 12-hour spine operation, heart attacks
and a stroke in ICU, an airlift to Milan, months relearning to stand, transfer,
and mechanically walk. In April 2024 Ricky and his twin brother founded Run4Ricky.
Now a team of friends runs marathons (London, Florence, Portofino) pushing Ricky's
wheelchair across the finish line, raising money for Spinal Research.

The through-line: **keep moving forward.** Momentum as literal fact and metaphor.

## Users (three journeys, all first-class)

1. **The donor** — moved by the story, wants to give and trust the money is used
   well. Needs: emotional hook, proof of impact, frictionless donate path.
2. **The newly-injured patient / their family** — searching for resources at the
   worst moment of their life. Needs: dignity, real guides (PDFs), practical aids,
   community. This page must never feel like an afterthought.
3. **The supporter / runner** — friends, followers, future teammates. Needs: events,
   team stories, media, easy sharing.

## Brand voice

Three words: **relentless, warm, unflinching.**

- Relentless: momentum verbs, present tense, forward motion. Never passive.
- Warm: this is people who love each other. Humor is allowed (the team is funny).
- Unflinching: don't sanitize the medical reality. Coma, stroke, ventilator. The
  honesty is what earns the hope.

Copy rules: every word earns its place. No em dashes. No corporate-charity mush
("empowering journeys of possibility"). Say the true, specific thing.

## Anti-references (what this must NOT look like)

- The generic white-and-teal "hopeful" nonprofit template. We deliberately went the
  other way: dark, electric, athletic.
- A hospital website. A pamphlet. A GoFundMe default.
- AI-slop landing page: centered stack, icon-title-subtitle card grid, gradient text,
  hero-metric template, pastel gradients.

## Strategic principles

- **Story first, ask second.** Earn the donation with the narrative before showing it.
- **Real over generated.** Ricky's real photos and videos carry the truth. AI imagery
  is atmospheric support only (kinetic orange/black textures, custom icons, illustrated
  timeline nodes). Never fabricate photos of Ricky or fake events.
- **Accessibility is the mission, not a checkbox.** This is a disability charity. WCAG
  AA minimum, real focus states, captions/controls on media, reduced-motion honored,
  keyboard-complete. An inaccessible SCI charity site is a self-own.
- **Stay vanilla.** Hand-written HTML/CSS/JS, no framework, no build step. Ships to
  GitHub Pages. Performance-conscious because the site is media-heavy.

## Constraints

- 5 pages: Home (index), Events & Updates, Media, Disabled Living, Donate.
- Keep 100% of existing content: photos, videos, PDFs, YouTube embeds, team stories.
- External links: PayPal, Spinal Research donation, social (FB/IG/YT), resource sites.
